What Each Dimension Covers
Plain English explanations — no jargon

Search — Can people find you on Google?

This measures how visible your website is when someone searches for champagne bars, cheese experiences, or event venues. It covers the technical setup of your site, the quality of your page content, how well your pages are structured, how trusted your site is, and whether you're targeting the right search terms.

Think of it as: if someone searched "champagne bar London" or "cheese and champagne tasting" right now, would they find you? A low score means the answer is probably no.

Design — Does your site look the part?

This assesses whether your website's visual presentation matches the quality and occasion-led nature of your venues. It covers layout and structure, visual identity, colour choices, typography, and animation. A premium experience with a mediocre-looking site loses credibility before a visitor reads a word.

Think of it as: if someone arrived at your site having seen your bar, would the website reinforce that same quality — or let it down?

Brand — Are you saying the right things, consistently?

This looks at whether your brand communicates clearly and consistently across your website. It covers the tone and quality of your writing, how visible you are across digital channels, how well you're positioned, and whether the experience feels joined-up across 6 locations.

Think of it as: does your website make it immediately obvious what makes Champagne+Fromage special — and does it say that confidently every time?

Compete — How do you compare to your competitors?

This maps your position in the market against direct competitors. It looks at what they do well, where they're vulnerable, what gap you could own, and whether you're differentiated enough for a buyer to choose you over them.

Think of it as: if someone looked at your site and a competitor's side by side, what would make them book with you — and is that obvious?

Growth — Is your site set up to win new business?

This evaluates whether your site has the infrastructure to attract visitors and convert them into bookings. It covers your ideal customer profile, your traffic strategy, your conversion copy, and the metrics you should be tracking to know what's working.

Think of it as: beyond people who already know you, is your website actively generating new bookings across all 6 locations? A low score means it probably isn't — and here's why.

Add LocalBusiness + Restaurant Schema to All 6 Location Pages

Each location page needs JSON-LD with: @type: ["LocalBusiness","Restaurant","BarOrPub"], name, address, telephone, openingHours, geo, servesCuisine, and a link to TripAdvisor as aggregateRating source.

Impact: Local Pack eligibility — currently zero chance of appearing in Google's 3-pack for any "champagne bar [city]" search
